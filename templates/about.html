OVERVIEW
THE PROBLEM
Why Does This Matter?
AI-generated images have become indistinguishable from real photographs to the human eye. Tools like Midjourney, DALL·E, and Stable Diffusion raise concerns around misinformation, copyright, and digital trust.

This classifier detects differences at a feature level — patterns the human eye simply cannot perceive.
APPROACH
Transfer Learning Strategy
Instead of training from scratch, we use models pre-trained on ImageNet — 1.2M images across 1,000 categories. These already know edges, textures, shapes, and complex visual patterns.

We then fine-tune them to learn subtle artefacts separating AI art from real photographs.

TRAINING PIPELINE
01
Image Collection & Validation
All images from AiArtData/ and RealArt/ scanned recursively. Corrupt files and images smaller than 10×10px detected and removed.
02
Data Split & tf.data Pipeline
Images shuffled and split 70/15/15. Lazy loading with augmentation: random flip, ±10% brightness, ±15% contrast. Cast to float32 — NO /255 division.
03
Phase 1 — Head Training
Backbone fully frozen. Only classification head trains: GAP → BatchNorm → Dense(512) → Dropout → Dense(128) → Sigmoid. LR: 1e-3.
04
Phase 2 — Surgical Fine-tuning
Only last 40 layers unfrozen. LR drops to 1e-5. Fresh EarlyStopping callback created per phase. Adjusts high-level feature extractors.
